Did You Know?


The Moon is a natural satellite of the Earth, and it is the fifth largest moon in the solar system. The Moon is the Earth's only natural satellite, and it is one of the largest satellites in the solar system, second only to Jupiter's four largest moons. The Moon's size relative to Earth is notable, as it is much larger than any other moon in relation to its host planet. In fact, the Moon is so large that it stabilizes Earth's axial tilt, which is responsible for our planet's moderate climate and seasonal changes.
