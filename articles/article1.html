About Nasa

NASA, or the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, is the United States government agency responsible for the country's civilian space program and for conducting research in aeronautics, space science, and related fields. NASA was established in 1958 in response to the Soviet Union's launch of the first artificial satellite, Sputnik, in 1957. Since then, NASA has been at the forefront of space exploration and has been responsible for many historic achievements, including the Apollo moon landing missions, the Space Shuttle program, the Hubble Space Telescope, and the International Space Station. NASA has also conducted numerous scientific missions to explore our solar system and beyond, using probes and rovers to study planets, asteroids, and comets. In addition to space exploration, NASA is also involved in Earth science research and studies our planet's climate, atmosphere, and natural resources. NASA's work has greatly advanced our understanding of the universe and has led to countless technological innovations that benefit society in areas such as medicine, transportation, and communications.
